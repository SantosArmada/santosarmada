Communication studies teaches that listening isn't passive — it's active work. Decoding meaning. Reading tone, pacing, even silence. Communication is a two-way process, happening in real time, and an interpreter has to live inside that process more fully than almost anyone else in the room. Interpreting isn't a speaking skill first. It's a listening skill first. From antique listening devices to modern headsets, the job has always been the same.
